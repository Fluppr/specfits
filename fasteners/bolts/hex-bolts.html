Hex bolts

A hex bolt has a six-sided head driven by a wrench or socket. It is the most common bolt type for structural and mechanical assemblies. Always use with a nut, or thread into a tapped hole.

Zinc-plated vs hot-dip galvanized vs stainless

  • Zinc-plated — indoor use; moderate short-term corrosion resistance
  • Hot-dip galvanized (HDG) — outdoor structural use, decks, bridges; thick zinc coat
  • Stainless steel (304/316) — marine, food service, outdoor decorative; no rust but lower strength than Grade 8
