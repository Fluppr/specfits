18650 battery reference

The 18650 is an 18 mm × 65 mm lithium-ion rechargeable cell. It is NOT a drop-in replacement for AA batteries — the voltage (3.6–3.7 V) is more than double that of an AA (1.5 V).

Specifications

PropertyValue
Diameter18.6 mm
Length65.2 mm (unprotected); up to 69 mm (protected)
Nominal voltage3.6–3.7 V
Fully charged voltage4.2 V
Minimum discharge voltage2.5–3.0 V (varies by cell)
Typical capacity1800–3500 mAh
ChemistryLithium-ion (Li-NMC, Li-NCA, LiFePO4 variants)
Charge rateTypically 0.5–1 C; check manufacturer spec
Cycle life300–500+ cycles to 80% capacity

Protected vs unprotected

TypeHas PCB circuit?LengthWhen to use
UnprotectedNo~65 mmDevices with built-in protection (most modern flashlights, laptops, EV packs)
ProtectedYes — prevents overcharge, over-discharge, short circuit~68–69 mmDevices without board-level protection, single-cell devices

Important: always check that a protected cell physically fits your device. The added PCB adds ~3–4 mm to length.

Flat-top vs button-top

TypePositive terminalUse
Flat-topFlush positive poleDevices with spring contacts or direct cell holders
Button-topSmall raised nub (~0.5–1 mm)Devices designed for AA-style cells or requiring positive contact clearance

Common devices using 18650

  • High-performance flashlights (most Fenix, Olight, Nitecore models)
  • Laptop battery packs (multi-cell)
  • Power banks (multi-cell)
  • Vaping/e-cigarette mods
  • Electric vehicle battery packs (Tesla Model S/3/X/Y, many others)
  • Cordless power tools (some models)

Safety notes

  • Only charge with a charger rated for Li-ion cells at the correct voltage
  • Never charge a damaged, dented, or leaking cell
  • Do not short circuit — Li-ion cells can deliver extremely high current and will overheat
  • Store at 40–60% charge if storing long-term
  • Do not dispose in household trash — recycle at a battery drop-off point
