9V battery reference

A 9V battery is a rectangular 9-volt cell with snap-style terminals on one end, commonly called a pp3 or 6F22.

Exact specs

Designation9V (IEC: 6LR61 alkaline, 6F22 carbon-zinc)
Width26.5 mm
Depth17.5 mm
Height48.5 mm
Nominal voltage9V
Typical capacity400–600 mAh (alkaline)
Terminal typeSnap connector (pos: small cap, neg: large ring)

View on Amazon

What devices commonly use it

Smoke detectors, guitar effects pedals, AM/FM radios, and some multimeters use 9V cells.

What it's interchangeable with

NiMH rechargeable 9V cells (8.4V nominal) work in most applications. Lithium 9V cells deliver more stable voltage and longer life in smoke detectors.

What it's NOT interchangeable with

The polarized snap connector physically prevents reverse insertion, but ensure terminal polarity matches the device's connector orientation.

Common mistakes

  • Snapping the battery in with reversed polarity awareness — snap connectors are keyed but always verify.
  • Assuming 9V cells last as long as AA in comparable jobs — capacity is much lower.
  • Leaving 9V cells in smoke detectors for more than a year without replacement.
